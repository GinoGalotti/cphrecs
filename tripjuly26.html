A summer trip on points · July 2026

London & Copenhagen

It's all booked. Three nights in London staying in Pimlico, two in Copenhagen staying out in Amager, home on the Wednesday. Below: the flights, the two bases, and — built around each one — where to eat, drink, browse and catch a gig.

From Stockholm (ARN) London Fri 3 → Mon 6 Jul · Pimlico Copenhagen Mon 6 → Wed 8 Jul · Amager Party 2 people

The plan

Fly into London on Fri 3 Jul for a three-night long weekend from a base by Victoria — markets, Soho, a couple of Ella-shaped nights out, and the splurge dinners of Pimlico on the doorstep. Then hop to Copenhagen on Mon 6 Jul for two slower, eating-focused nights from an Amager flat near the beach, before the early flight home on Wed 8 Jul.

The Copenhagen leg also leans on the site's own eating guide.

Breakfast & brunch · near the base

Pimlico Fresh

Wilton Rd · 5-min walk

Tiny indie café with a blackboard of porridge, French toast and sourdough — the local favourite.

Timmy Green

Bright, airy Aussie all-day brunch — corn fritters, big windows, a terrace, right by the station.

The English Rose Café

Family-run independent doing a proper full English a stone's throw from the door.

Daylesford Organic

Smart farm-to-table café for organic eggs, pastries and a more upmarket weekend brunch.

Lunch & dinner

Pubs & live music

The Queens Arms

Warwick Way, Pimlico · 5-min walk

Classic 1840s corner local for real ale and pub dinners — basically around the corner.

The Cask Pub & Kitchen

Charlwood St, Pimlico · 10-min walk

Superb craft-beer and cask-ale backstreet pub — the best beer destination near the base.

The 100 Club

Oxford St · short tube

Legendary 1942 basement that helped birth UK punk, still booking grassroots rock and punk.

The Underworld

Camden · tube north

Sweaty 500-cap basement — London's go-to for punk, hardcore and metal almost nightly.

More grassroots gigs for Ella: The Windmill (Brixton) and MOTH Club (Hackney) — both worth checking listings for 3–5 Jul.

Vintage & second-hand · the scene

Beyond Retro

Soho · ~10-min tube

Huge curated warehouse of vintage Americana, denim and party wear — the closest serious vintage.

Rokit Vintage

Covent Garden · ~10-min tube

Long-running vintage shop strong on reworked denim, band tees and 80s/90s pieces.

Brick Lane Vintage Market

Shoreditch (East) · a trek

Basement of ~100 dealers — the best concentration of alt/punk vintage in London.

Camden Market

Camden (North) · ~20-min tube

Stalls of punk, goth, band merch and one-off vintage — the spiritual home of the alt scene.

For Ella

Beyond the gigs and bars above — the Pokémon hunt and the proper cocktails. (Live music is in “Around each base”: 100 Club, Underworld, Windmill in London; Loppen, Stengade in Copenhagen.)

Pokémon & collectibles · London

Brotherhood Games Café

Bermondsey

Best TCG spot in London per the regulars — play Pokémon over food, fair prices, great events.

Fanatics Collectibles, Regent St

Regent Street

Big central TCG store — cards, sealed product, singles. Expect a managed queue at busy times.

Forbidden Planet

Shaftesbury Ave

Two floors of pop-culture everything — Pokémon plush, figures and merch alongside manga.

The official Pokémon Center pop-ups ran Feb–April 2026 and are closed by July — the shops above are the year-round options. In Copenhagen, try Faraos Cigarer and Bog & Idé for cards.

Cocktails · London

Swift, Soho

Soho

A serious cocktail bar locals rate — best Irish coffee in London, expert twists on classics.

Cahoots, Carnaby

Carnaby

Immersive 1940s-Underground theme with live music — drinks in lanterns and tube carriages.

SOMA, Soho

Soho

A genuine speakeasy — clear ice, freezer glassware, no fruit-salad garnishes. The real thing.

Cocktails · Copenhagen

Ruby

Indre By

Copenhagen's benchmark cocktail bar — a canal-side townhouse, faultless classics, easy-going.

Lidkoeb

Vesterbro

Candle-lit three-floor bar in a courtyard — top floor is a whisky room. A proper Copenhagen night.
