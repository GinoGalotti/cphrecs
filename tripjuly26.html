A long weekend on points · July 2026

London or Amsterdam

Two cities, two sets of dates, both reachable from Arlanda on SAS EuroBonus points. Here's the side-by-side — flights, places to stay, and what's worth doing — so we can just pick one.

From Stockholm (ARN) London Wed 1 → Sat 4 Jul Amsterdam Fri 3 → Tue 7 Jul Party 2 people

The short version

London is the bucket-list pick, but the cheap award seats sit at the awkward ends of the day: the only 15k-point outbound is the 19:45 departure (lands ~21:20), and the matching cheap return is early-to-mid afternoon. That's really two full days on the ground.

Amsterdam is now the easier trip on every axis — friendlier flight times and, if you extend one night to Tue 7 Jul, a direct 15k return that beats London's awkward afternoon slot. Smaller city, more relaxed, very walkable. London is still the bucket-list pick if you want the big-city hit; Amsterdam wins on low-stress logistics and a fuller stay.

04

What's worth doing

A menu, not a schedule — pick what appeals. Grouped by mood so we can mix landmarks, culture and food to taste.

Landmarks & views

Tower of London

Crown Jewels and the Beefeater tour. Arrive at 9am opening to beat the queues. 4.7★

Sky Garden

Free rooftop garden with the best City skyline view — book a timed slot ahead. 4.6★

Westminster walk

Big Ben, the Abbey, the bridges. Self-guided and free, best in the evening light.

Museums & galleries · all free

British Museum

Rosetta Stone, Egypt, Greece. Vast — pick two wings rather than all of it. 4.7★

Tate Modern

A power station on the river; ride to the 10th-floor view. 4.5★

National Gallery

Trafalgar Square — Van Gogh, Turner, Whistlejacket. 4.8★

Food & nights out

Borough Market

Open Thu–Sat — perfect for our dates. Graze breakfast, go early. 4.6★

Soho & Covent Garden

Bars, restaurants, Neal's Yard — likely our hotel base anyway.

A West End show

Book ahead, or grab same-day deals at TKTS in Leicester Square.

Canals & on foot

Jordaan wander

The prettiest quarter — canals, indie shops, brown cafés. No plan needed, just walk.

Canal cruise

Touristy but genuinely the best way to read the city. Go for a small-boat evening one.

Vondelpark

The big central park — rent bikes, or run it in the morning. Free.

Museums & culture

Rijksmuseum

Rembrandt, Vermeer and the Dutch Masters. Book a timed slot. 4.8★

Van Gogh Museum

The world's largest Van Gogh collection, next door to the Rijks. Book ahead — it sells out.

Anne Frank House

Sobering and essential. Tickets release on a strict timed schedule — book the moment they open.

Food & neighbourhoods

Albert Cuyp Market

De Pijp's street market — stroopwafels made fresh, cheese, herring if you're brave.

De Pijp & Oost

Best eating-and-drinking neighbourhoods, away from the centre's crowds.

A brown café evening

Cosy old Dutch pubs — the proper way to spend a slow night.

05

For Ella

The good stuff: small gigs (punk/rock by preference), anything Pokémon-flavoured, and proper cocktails. Live music is the one to check closer to the date — small venues book weeks out, so the spots below are where to look rather than confirmed shows.

Small gigs · punk & rock

The Windmill, Brixton

The legendary South London grassroots room — cheap, sweaty, and where tomorrow's bands play tonight. Check listings for early July. 4.5★

MOTH Club, Hackney

A converted working men's club with a glittery gold stage. Intimate, brilliant for up-and-comers. (Currently fighting redevelopment — go support it.) 4.5★

Hootananny, Brixton

Pub-venue with live bands and a loud, friendly crowd most nights. Reggae, ska, punk-leaning. 4.3★

Pokémon & collectibles

Brotherhood Games Café

Best TCG spot in London per the regulars — play Pokémon over food, fair prices, great events. Bermondsey, a little out but worth it. 4.8★

Fanatics Collectibles, Regent St

Big central TCG store — cards, sealed product, singles. Expect a managed queue at busy times. 4.7★

Forbidden Planet, Shaftesbury Ave

Two floors of pop-culture everything — Pokémon plush, figures, merch alongside manga and comics. 4.6★

Heads up: the official Pokémon Center pop-ups (ExCeL and the Natural History Museum) ran Feb–April 2026 and are closed by July. No permanent Pokémon Center in London — the shops above are the year-round options. POP MART on Oxford St is also fun for blind-box collectibles if that's her thing.

Cocktails

Swift, Soho

A serious cocktail bar that locals rate — best Irish coffee in London, expert twists on classics. Book if you can. 4.6★

Cahoots, Carnaby

Immersive 1940s-Underground-station theme with live music — drinks served in lanterns and tube carriages. Fun over purist. 4.6★

SOMA, Soho

A genuine speakeasy — clear ice, freezer glassware, no fruit-salad garnishes. For when she wants the real thing. 4.7★

Small gigs · punk & rock

Vrankrijk

A 40-year-old legal squat off Spuistraat — proper underground punk gigs, dirt-cheap drinks, friendly crowd. Cash only. The real deal. 4.7★

The Cave Rock Club

Tiny metal/rock bar near the centre with a built-in stage and live Dutch bands at weekends. Loud, cosy, welcoming. 4.7★

The Waterhole

Dive-bar aesthetic near Leidseplein with live rock most nights and a small entry fee. Pool tables, happy hour. 4.5★

Pokémon & collectibles

The Gamekeeper, Jordaan

Beautifully old-school family-run toy/game shop on Hartenstraat with a lovely Pokémon collection. Central, in your neighbourhood. 4.4★

Amsterdam Toys (Duivendrecht)

Regulars call it the best TCG store in the Netherlands — great prices, strong anti-scalper rules. A metro ride out, open Wed–Sat. 4.8★

Collectableworld, Kalverstraat

Central card shop with a big Pokémon selection and singles. Pricier on sealed product — good for hunting specific cards. 3.5★

Cocktails

Sins of Sal, Jordaan

Creative, intentional cocktails and Latin small plates right in the Jordaan. Warm crew, great vibe — book a day ahead. 4.8★

Bar Oldenhof, Jordaan

Cosy, classic craft-cocktail bar — bartenders build custom drinks around what you like. Neighbourhood gem. 4.8★

Belly of the Beast

Often called the best cocktail bar in the city — unique menu, chill atmosphere, the kind of place you return to three nights running. 4.5★
