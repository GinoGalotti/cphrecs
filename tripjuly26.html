03

What's worth doing

A menu, not a schedule — pick what appeals. London gets the long weekend; Copenhagen is two easy days, mostly built around eating (see the guide in section 05).

Landmarks & views

Tower of London

Crown Jewels and the Beefeater tour. Arrive at 9am opening to beat the queues. 4.7★

Sky Garden

Free rooftop garden with the best City skyline view — book a timed slot ahead. 4.6★

Westminster walk

Big Ben, the Abbey, the bridges. Self-guided and free, best in the evening light.

Museums & galleries · all free

British Museum

Rosetta Stone, Egypt, Greece. Vast — pick two wings rather than all of it. 4.7★

Tate Modern

A power station on the river; ride to the 10th-floor view. 4.5★

National Gallery

Trafalgar Square — Van Gogh, Turner, Whistlejacket. 4.8★

Food & nights out

Borough Market

Open Thu–Sat — perfect for our Fri/Sat. Graze breakfast, go early. 4.6★

Soho & Covent Garden

Bars, restaurants, Neal's Yard — the natural evening base.

A West End show

Book ahead, or grab same-day deals at TKTS in Leicester Square.

Two easy days

Nyhavn & the harbour

The postcard canal, then a harbour-bus or walk along the water. Free, and the obvious first stroll.

Tivoli Gardens

Right by Central Station — historic pleasure garden, lovely in the evening. Good for our last night.

Rosenborg & the King's Garden

Renaissance castle, crown jewels, and the city's nicest park to sit in.

Neighbourhoods & eating

Torvehallerne

Glass food-hall by Nørreport — smørrebrød, coffee, snacks. A great grazing lunch.

Reffen / Refshaleøen

Sprawling street-food yard on the harbour island — go for an evening, bring an appetite.

Nørrebro & Vesterbro

The two best wander-and-eat neighbourhoods. The eating guide covers these in depth.

04

For Ella · in London

The good stuff: small gigs (punk/rock by preference), anything Pokémon-flavoured, and proper cocktails. Live music is the one to check closer to the date — small venues book weeks out, so the spots below are where to look rather than confirmed shows.

Small gigs · punk & rock

The Windmill, Brixton

The legendary South London grassroots room — cheap, sweaty, and where tomorrow's bands play tonight. Check listings for early July. 4.5★

MOTH Club, Hackney

A converted working men's club with a glittery gold stage. Intimate, brilliant for up-and-comers. 4.5★

Hootananny, Brixton

Pub-venue with live bands and a loud, friendly crowd most nights. Reggae, ska, punk-leaning. 4.3★

If we land at The Bedford in Balham, the gig venue is literally downstairs — check who's playing 3–5 Jul.

Pokémon & collectibles

Brotherhood Games Café

Best TCG spot in London per the regulars — play Pokémon over food, fair prices, great events. Bermondsey. 4.8★

Fanatics Collectibles, Regent St

Big central TCG store — cards, sealed product, singles. Expect a managed queue at busy times. 4.7★

Forbidden Planet, Shaftesbury Ave

Two floors of pop-culture everything — Pokémon plush, figures, merch alongside manga and comics. 4.6★

Heads up: the official Pokémon Center pop-ups (ExCeL and the Natural History Museum) ran Feb–April 2026 and are closed by July. The shops above are the year-round options. POP MART on Oxford St is also fun for blind-box collectibles.

Cocktails

Swift, Soho

A serious cocktail bar that locals rate — best Irish coffee in London, expert twists on classics. Book if you can. 4.6★

Cahoots, Carnaby

Immersive 1940s-Underground-station theme with live music — drinks served in lanterns and tube carriages. 4.6★

SOMA, Soho

A genuine speakeasy — clear ice, freezer glassware, no fruit-salad garnishes. For the real thing. 4.7★
